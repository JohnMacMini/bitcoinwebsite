Survey questions

Bitcoin policy questions used for verified office outreach.

These are the standardized questions used when campaigns and congressional offices provide direct responses. The questions are public for transparency, but official responses are not collected through an open public web form.

Verification note

  • Responses should come from an official office or campaign representative.
  • Open public submissions are not treated as official office responses.
  • The question set remains public so voters can see the exact standard used.

Questionnaire

Standard questions for a clear Bitcoin position

Core questions

  1. Is Bitcoin distinct from the broader crypto market?
  2. Does the office support the right to self-custody Bitcoin?
  3. Does the office support lawful Bitcoin mining in the United States?
  4. Does the office support Bitcoin-specific federal legislation if aligned with its principles?
  5. What is the office’s current overall position on Bitcoin?

Optional clarification

Offices may also provide an optional open statement explaining nuance, caveats, priorities, or distinctions between Bitcoin and the broader crypto market.

Verification process

To submit an official response, reply through an official congressional or campaign communication channel and reference these survey questions.
